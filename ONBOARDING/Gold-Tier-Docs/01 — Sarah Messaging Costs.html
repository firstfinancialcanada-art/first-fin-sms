Sarah Messaging Costs

Plain-English breakdown of what your dealership pays for Sarah's SMS and voice usage on the Gold tier subscription. Designed to be readable by anyone, not just the manager.

The short version

Your $225/month Gold subscription includes $18.50 of Sarah messaging credit. That's enough for a typical dealership's normal lead-handling load. Heavy month? You can pre-purchase top-up credit anytime — it never expires.

What "$18.50" actually means in practice: roughly 50–100 customer conversations per month, where a "conversation" = a customer's initial text + Sarah's reply + a couple back-and-forths. Most dealerships don't get close to the cap unless they're running aggressive paid lead campaigns.

What does a "typical conversation" cost?

A back-and-forth where a customer texts asking about a vehicle and Sarah handles the qualification (10–20 messages total) runs roughly 20–40¢. The included $18.50 covers about 50–100 of these per month.

How to track usage

From inside the platform, click the "Usage" pill in the top header bar. You'll see:

  • Total messaging spend this month — broken down by SMS vs. voice
  • Bar meter — green under 80%, amber 80–99%, red at or over the included credit
  • Reset date — first of next month
  • Top-up balance — any pre-purchased credit you've added

Only managers and owners see the Usage panel. Sales reps don't need to think about cost; they just talk to customers.

What happens when you hit the cap?

At 80% used: the meter turns amber. No interruption to service — just a heads up that you're heading toward the included limit.
At 100% used (no top-up): Sarah pauses outbound replies until either (a) you top up, or (b) the next billing month rolls over. Customer messages still arrive in your CRM — your reps can reply manually.
At 100% used (with top-up balance): overflow seamlessly draws from your top-up balance. No interruption.

Why the per-message price isn't a fixed number

Messaging carriers (Twilio under the hood) charge different rates for:

  • Number of message segments (160 characters each — short messages = 1 segment, long ones split)
  • Destination country (Canada is cheap, US is similar, international is more)
  • Message type (regular SMS vs. MMS with photos, voice minutes vs. SMS)

The platform reconciles the actual cost from carrier callbacks — your meter shows the real number, not an estimate.

Your one Sarah number serves the whole dealership

Each Gold-tier dealership gets one local Twilio number. All inbound texts and calls land in your shared CRM, where they auto-route to the right rep based on your routing rules. The $18.50 messaging credit covers all 10 of your team members combined — there's no per-rep cap.

Quick FAQ

Can I see who's using the most messaging?

Yes — in the Team panel, the activity view shows messaging volume per rep over the last 30 days.

Can I cap individual reps' usage?

Not currently — the cap is per-tenant (whole dealership). If a single rep is burning through the credit, you'll see it in the Team panel and can address it directly.

Do incoming messages from customers count against the credit?

Yes — both incoming and outgoing messages have a small per-message cost. Incoming is typically the cheaper of the two (about 1¢ each).

What happens if I downgrade from Gold?

Your top-up balance survives the downgrade and continues to apply. You just lose the seats and the included $18.50.
