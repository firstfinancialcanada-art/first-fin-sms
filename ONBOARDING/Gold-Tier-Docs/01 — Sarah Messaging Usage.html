Sarah Messaging Usage

Plain-English breakdown of how Sarah's SMS and voice usage works on the Gold tier subscription. Designed to be readable by anyone, not just the manager.

The short version

Your $225/month Gold subscription includes a monthly messaging allowance sized for a typical dealership's lead-handling volume. Most dealerships never come close to using all of it. Heavy month? You can pre-purchase top-up credit anytime — it never expires.

What "the included allowance" means in practice: enough for a typical dealership's normal monthly lead volume — initial customer texts, Sarah's qualification replies, and several back-and-forths per lead. Most dealerships don't approach the cap unless they're running aggressive paid lead campaigns.

What does a "typical conversation" use?

A back-and-forth where a customer texts asking about a vehicle and Sarah handles the qualification is a small fraction of your monthly allowance. Most dealers find the included allowance covers their normal monthly volume comfortably without ever needing to top up.

How to track usage

From inside the platform, click the "Usage" pill in the top header bar. You'll see:

  • Bar meter — green under 80%, amber 80–99%, red at or over the included allowance
  • Reset date — first of next month
  • Top-up balance — any pre-purchased credit you've added
  • Spend breakdown — SMS vs. voice usage

Only managers and owners see the Usage panel. Sales reps don't need to think about cost; they just talk to customers.

What happens when you hit the cap?

At 80% used: the meter turns amber. No interruption to service — just a heads up that you're heading toward the included limit.
At 100% used (no top-up): Sarah pauses outbound replies until either (a) you top up, or (b) the next billing month rolls over. Customer messages still arrive in your CRM — your reps can reply manually.
At 100% used (with top-up balance): overflow seamlessly draws from your top-up balance. No interruption.

Top-up pricing

If you exceed the included allowance, pre-purchased credit applies automatically. Buy in any amount, anytime, via the Usage panel — $10, $25, $50, or $100 increments. Each tier extends your messaging capacity proportionally; the bigger the top-up, the more conversation runway you get.

Top-up balance carries forward month-to-month and never expires.

Why pricing isn't a fixed per-message number

Messaging carriers charge different rates for:

  • Number of message segments (160 characters each — short messages = 1 segment, long ones split)
  • Destination country (Canada is cheap, US is similar, international is more)
  • Message type (regular SMS vs. MMS with photos, voice minutes vs. SMS)

The platform reconciles actual cost from carrier callbacks — your meter shows the real volume, not an estimate.

Your one Sarah number serves the whole dealership

Each Gold-tier dealership gets one local Twilio number. All inbound texts and calls land in your shared CRM, where they auto-route to the right rep based on your routing rules. The included messaging allowance covers all 10 of your team members combined — there's no per-rep cap.

Quick FAQ

Can I see who's using the most messaging?

Yes — in the Team panel, the activity view shows messaging volume per rep over the last 30 days.

Can I cap individual reps' usage?

Not currently — the cap is per-tenant (whole dealership). If a single rep is burning through the credit, you'll see it in the Team panel and can address it directly.

Do incoming messages from customers count against the allowance?

Yes — both incoming and outgoing messages count, though each individual message is a very small portion of the monthly total.

What happens if I downgrade from Gold?

Your top-up balance survives the downgrade and continues to apply. You just lose the seats and the included messaging allowance.
