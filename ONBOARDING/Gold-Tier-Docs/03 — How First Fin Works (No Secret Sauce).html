How First Fin Works

Plain-English breakdown of every Gold-tier feature, what it does, and the assumptions behind it. No secret sauce. If you ask "how does Sarah really work?" or "what data does First Fin own?" — the answers are below.

The platform in 30 seconds

First Fin is a web-based dealership operating system that handles four things end-to-end: lead intake (from AutoTrader / Kijiji / CarCostCanada / TAQ), customer follow-up (Sarah AI texting + voice), deal desking (lender comparison + structuring), and marketplace listings (Facebook Marketplace auto-fill). Your dealership data lives in your tenant — isolated from every other dealer on the platform.

Feature 1 — Inventory Importer

What it does

A Chrome extension that scans your dealer website's inventory page and syncs every vehicle to First Fin — VIN, stock #, year/make/model/trim, price, mileage, color, condition, and up to 25 photos per vehicle.

How it works (no magic)

  • Reads the HTML of your existing inventory page (no special API needed)
  • Recognizes 6 dealer-platform formats: Convertus/Achilles (Stellantis), Sunridge Auto, eDealer, D2C Media, House of Cars, and generic VDP-link sites
  • For Convertus dealers: opens each VDP page, clicks "View Photos" to populate the lightbox, scrapes the photo gallery URLs (5–7 minutes for 79 vehicles)
  • Sends scraped data to your tenant via authenticated API call — no third party sees it

Limits

  • 1,000 vehicles per dealership (lifts to 2,500 on Enterprise tier)
  • 25 photos per vehicle (Facebook's per-listing limit is 20; we fetch 25 so reps can pick the best)
  • Re-scan anytime — duplicate VINs/stock numbers skip cleanly (ADD mode)

What we don't do

  • We don't use the dealer's CMS credentials
  • We don't access dealer's CRM or DMS
  • We don't share inventory data across tenants

Feature 2 — Lead Intake (ADF / XML)

What it does

Each tenant gets a unique email address (e.g., huntchrysler@firstfinancialcanada.com). Paste it into AutoTrader / Kijiji / CarCostCanada / TAQ as a lead recipient. Every inbound lead auto-creates a CRM row and routes to one of your reps based on rules you set.

How it works

  • Lead provider sends an ADF (Auto-Lead Data Format) XML email to your dealership-specific address
  • Gmail Workspace forwards it to a single IMAP-monitored mailbox
  • First Fin's poller reads the inbox every 60 seconds, parses the ADF body, identifies the destination tenant by To: address
  • Creates a CRM row with customer name, phone (E.164 normalized), email, vehicle interest, source, and full ADF metadata in notes
  • Applies your routing rules → assigns to a rep → SMS notifies the rep

  • We don't sell or share leads with anyone
  • We don't auto-reply on the dealer's behalf without explicit routing rules

Feature 3 — Sarah (AI SMS responder)

What it does

Sarah is your dealership's after-hours and high-volume SMS responder. She handles initial qualification ("what's your budget?", "trade-in?", "test drive when?"), books appointments, and hands off warm leads to a human rep with full conversation context.

How it works

  • Each tenant has one dedicated local phone number for Sarah (auto-provisioned during the setup wizard)
  • Inbound SMS hits our messaging gateway → forwards to Sarah's reasoning loop
  • Sarah uses an LLM (Claude or similar) with a strict dealership-trained prompt
  • Sarah's reply persona is your dealership — "Sarah from {Your Dealership}"
  • Conversation persists in CRM; reps can pick up mid-thread
  • Sarah escalates to a human rep when: customer asks for a test drive, customer asks a price/finance question outside her authority, or after 3 back-and-forths

Cost model

See the separate Sarah Messaging Usage document for details. TLDR: monthly messaging allowance included with the $225/mo Gold subscription (sized for typical dealership volume), top-ups available, no per-rep cap.

What we don't do

  • Sarah doesn't speak to a customer pretending to be a real human — she introduces herself as an AI assistant
  • Sarah doesn't make pricing or finance commitments — she escalates to a rep
  • Sarah doesn't share customer data across tenants — each dealer's conversations are isolated
  • Sarah doesn't store credit card or banking info — we route customers to your existing finance application links

Feature 4 — Deal Desk + Lender Comparison

What it does

Structure a deal (selling price, trade, term, rate) and instantly compare side-by-side payment scenarios across every major Canadian auto lender. Includes hard-limit checks, doc fees, and PDF export for the customer.

Lender data sources

  • Hard-limit lenders: published lender rate sheets (refreshed manually, editable per dealership in the Rates panel)
  • Credit-based lenders (CIBC, RBC, etc.): use Max LTV from public bank rate posts
  • Each manager can override default rates for their own dealership (e.g., your buy-rate spread)

What we don't do

  • We don't pull customer credit (no Equifax/TransUnion integration)
  • We don't submit applications to lenders directly — that's still your finance manager's job
  • We don't share rate overrides across tenants

Feature 5 — Facebook Marketplace Auto-Post

What it does

From the FB Poster panel, click Auto-Fill Facebook on any vehicle. A second Chrome tab opens to FB Marketplace's "create vehicle listing" form, pre-filled with year/make/model/trim/price/mileage/body style/condition/description, and the 25 vehicle photos pre-uploaded.

How it works

  • Chrome extension opens FB Marketplace create-vehicle URL
  • Injects a script that fills each form field via the field's React state
  • Photos are downloaded as base64 in the extension's background, then uploaded into FB's photo input
  • Rep reviews the listing, removes any unwanted photos (FB's cap is 20, we send 25), clicks "Publish" themselves

What we don't do

  • We don't auto-publish — the rep clicks the final Post button. This keeps you compliant with FB's Terms of Service.
  • We don't store FB credentials. We don't log into FB on your behalf. The rep is logged into their own FB account.
  • We don't crosspost to other marketplaces (Kijiji, etc.) without manual setup

Branding & Watermarking

Where your branding shows up

  • Sarah's SMS signature — "— Sarah from {Your Dealership}"
  • FB Marketplace listings — title prefix + description footer mention your dealership name
  • Deal desk PDFs — your logo top-left, dealership address footer
  • Customer-facing emails (pre-qualification follow-ups, deal recaps) — branded to your dealership
  • Equifax / TD pre-qualify widget — co-branded with your dealership name

What we don't watermark

  • Vehicle photos — they go to FB Marketplace as-is, no First Fin watermark
  • Customer-facing pages don't carry "Powered by First Fin" branding (white-label by default)

Data ownership & privacy

What we collect

  • Inventory data you import (VIN/stock/photos/etc.) — yours, exportable on request
  • Lead data sent to your intake email (name, phone, email, vehicle interest)
  • Sarah conversation history (SMS bodies + timestamps)
  • Deal desk activity (which deals were structured, by which rep, when)
  • Usage telemetry (which features get used, by which rep, how often) — for product improvement only, not sold

What we don't collect

  • Customer credit reports / credit scores
  • Customer banking information
  • Customer driver's license images or government IDs
  • Anything in your DMS, accounting system, or other dealer software

Where the data lives

  • Postgres database hosted on Railway (Canadian region preferred for Canadian dealers)
  • Photos hosted on Convertus / Autotrader CDN (we link, we don't rehost)
  • Sarah's reasoning runs on a leading enterprise LLM provider — your data is never used for model training
  • Our messaging gateway handles SMS / voice — message bodies are retained for 30 days for delivery debugging only

If you cancel

  • 30 days notice via Stripe customer portal
  • Data export (CSV / JSON) available on request, within 7 days
  • After 90 days post-cancellation, all tenant data is permanently deleted from our DB and backups

Multi-tenant isolation (how 10 dealerships don't see each other)

Every database row that holds dealership data carries a tenant_id column. Every read and write query filters by the authenticated user's tenant. This isn't a configuration setting — it's enforced in the API layer for every endpoint. Auditable in the source code on request (NDA basis).

Specifically isolated:

  • Inventory — Hunt's reps cannot see Milton's lot, even by guessing URL paths
  • CRM — leads route only to the destination tenant
  • Sarah conversations — Sarah greets each customer with the dealership name they messaged
  • Settings — branding, lead intake email, lender rate overrides — per-tenant only
  • Sarah numbers — each tenant gets their own dedicated local number
