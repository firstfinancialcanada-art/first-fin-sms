05 — CASL & SMS Compliance
CASL & SMS Compliance
5.1 — CASL Overview

Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation

Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation (CASL) requires that commercial electronic messages (CEMs), including SMS messages, be sent only with the recipient's express or implied consent, contain sender identification, and include an unsubscribe mechanism.

Your Responsibility: You are solely responsible for ensuring that all contacts in your CRM have provided the necessary consent before using the Sarah AI SMS feature to contact them. First Financial Canada provides the tools — compliance with CASL is the responsibility of each subscriber.
5.2 — Implied Consent

Implied Consent

Implied consent exists where there is an existing business relationship (e.g., a customer who recently inquired about a vehicle). Implied consent is time-limited under CASL and should not be relied upon indefinitely. We recommend obtaining express written consent where possible.

5.3 — Unsubscribe

Unsubscribe Requests

Any contact who replies STOP, UNSUBSCRIBE, or equivalent to an SMS sent through the Platform will be automatically removed from further AI-initiated outreach immediately and in real time — no manual action is required. The Platform processes opt-out requests automatically upon receipt.

As required by CASL, all unsubscribe requests must be honoured within 10 business days. The Platform exceeds this requirement by processing them instantly. You remain responsible for ensuring that any manual outreach outside the Platform also respects opt-out records.
